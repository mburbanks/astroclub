Predicted Tides at Baltimore (full 2026).

These Tide Predictions are computed from tide-height data collected at random times at Baltimore Pier since 2024. Sea-level heights were established by photographing the steel ladders at the Pier, using them as a de facto measurement scale. Pressure was also recorded, as mean sea level varies approximately 1cm for every unit of pressure.

Combining the height and pressure data with the then astronomical positions of the Sun and Moon allows a set of harmonic constants (wave amplitudes and phase-lags) to be derived for the various tidal constituents, allowing the model to provide a “best fit” to the data collected. The resulting harmonic constants serve as a unique "characteristic" of the tide at the Pier, and can be used to predict. As more data gets collected, the more accurate the model becomes.
